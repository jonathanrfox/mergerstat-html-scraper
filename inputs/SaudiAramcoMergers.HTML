Deal Description:

Shell Oil Co., a unit of the Royal Dutch/ Shell Group and Saudi Refining, a unit of Aramco Services and the Saudi Arabian Oil Co., acquired Motiva Enterprises from Texaco for an undisclosed price. This acquisition would expand upon the global distribution services of petroleum products. Both the Royal Dutch/Shell Group and the Saudi Arabian Oil Co will each maintain a 50% interest in Motiva Enterprises. In connection with this transaction, the Royal Dutch/Shell Group agreed to acquire the remaining 44% interest in Equilon Enterprises from Texaco. Together, the transactions have a combined value of $2.3 billion in cash, $1.6 billion in debt, and $300 million in pension liabilities. Under the terms of the two transactions, Equilon Enterprises and Motiva Enterprises will have exclusive use of the Texaco brand for until June 2004. Motiva Enterprises operates primarily in the eastern United States, and includes approximately 4,800 Shell gas stations and 8,200 Texaco station and four refineries. Equilon Enterprises operates primarily in the western United States and has approximately 4,500 Shell stations and 4,500 Texaco stations, and four refineries.
